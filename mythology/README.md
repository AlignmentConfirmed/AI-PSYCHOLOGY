# Mythology — How AI Interprets 18 World Cultures

The researcher prompted AI systems to explore how different
cultural and mythological traditions shaped their people's
understanding of the world. The AI generated one document
per tradition — 18 in total.

## What's Here

Aboriginal, Arabic, Babel, Chinese, Egyptian, English,
Greek, Hebrew, Japanese, Latin, Mayan, Norse, Russian,
Sanskrit, Sumerian, Swahili, Ukrainian, Vedic.

Each document explores what a cultural tradition emphasized
and what it left in shadow. These are comparative exercises
in how different peoples encoded their observations about
reality into transmissible narratives.

## What This Shows

AI produces remarkably consistent structural analysis across
all 18 traditions — finding the same pattern everywhere: a
culture identifies something real, encodes it in narrative,
and over time the narrative becomes the authority rather than
the underlying observation.

Whether this pattern is genuinely universal or an artifact
of the AI's own training data bias is an open question. The
AI was trained on text about all of these cultures. It may
be finding patterns in its training data rather than in the
cultures themselves.

## Important Notes

- These are NOT rankings of cultural value or truth
- These are NOT religious commentary or critique
- These are NOT anthropological scholarship
- Each tradition contains beauty, wisdom, and genuine insight
- The AI's analysis is ONE lens, shaped by its training data
- Native speakers and cultural practitioners are better
  authorities on their own traditions than any AI

*These are comparative exercises. Read them with curiosity
and humility. Every culture deserves better than an AI's
